About Asset

Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency that operates on a peer-to-peer network, allowing users to send and receive payments without the need for a central authority like a bank or government. It was introduced in 2008 by an unknown person or group using the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto. Bitcoin transactions are recorded on a public ledger called the blockchain, which ensures transparency and security.

The supply of Bitcoin is capped at 21 million coins, making it a deflationary asset. It is created through a process called mining, where powerful computers solve complex mathematical problems to validate transactions and secure the network, earning new bitcoins as a reward. Bitcoin has gained popularity as both a form of digital money and a store of value, often compared to gold due to its limited supply and decentralized nature.

However, its price is highly volatile, and it has faced regulatory challenges and skepticism from some financial institutions. Despite this, Bitcoin has inspired a broader cryptocurrency movement, leading to the creation of thousands of alternative digital currencies.
